# Phase 3 — MVP Hardening & Beta Preparation

## Phase purpose

Phase 3 hardens the MVP, proves the core loop through founder testing, prepares Big Al for invited users, then runs a controlled private beta before any wider launch decision.

This phase is not about expanding Big Al into new product areas. It is about making the existing product reliable, understandable and useful enough for real Restaurants.

## Current starting point

Phase 3 begins after M14 technical completion and founder UAT handoff.

Current product state:

- M0-M14 are built on `clean-milestone-4-sync`.
- The core loop exists: Import → Save → Plan → Shop → Cook.
- Founder UAT is in progress.
- Launch sign-off is not complete.
- Remaining work is UAT closeout, reliability fixes and beta preparation.

## Phase rule

Do not start new feature areas during Phase 3 unless they directly unblock founder UAT or private beta.

Big Al must stay focused on making cooking easy and enjoyable.

## Do not build in Phase 3

- public social feeds
- followers or following
- likes or view counts
- influencer mechanics
- grocery price comparison
- retailer integrations
- barcode scanning
- full pantry inventory management
- calorie tracking
- nutrition tracking
- smart appliance integrations
- voice cooking assistant
- generic chatbot behaviour
- paid AI integration unless explicitly approved later

## Success criteria for Phase 3

Phase 3 is successful when:

- the founder can complete Import → Save → Plan → Shop → Cook in the live app without intervention
- duplicate imports do not create accidental recipe clutter
- Pantry produces a list that feels practical enough to shop from
- Cook Mode can be used to cook a full recipe without returning to the original source
- beta testers understand what Big Al does without personal explanation
- data remains Restaurant-scoped and safe
- launch blockers are clearly separated from nice-to-have polish
- a clear beta or launch decision can be made from evidence, not vibes

## Phase exit decision

At the end of M22, decide one of:

1. Expand private beta.
2. Run another hardening cycle.
3. Prepare for soft launch.
4. Pause and rework part of the core loop.

Do not move to wider launch without explicit founder approval.
